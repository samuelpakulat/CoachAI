# Finding remote jobs on LinkedIn (by hand)

Run this alongside the automated tracker. The tracker covers job-board APIs;
LinkedIn you do manually because it blocks bots. The whole point is to spend
~10 minutes a day and let LinkedIn email you the rest.

## 1. Set up one good search

1. Go to **linkedin.com/jobs**.
2. In the **keyword** box, search one role at a time (do a few):
   - `customer support`
   - `customer success`
   - `virtual assistant`
   - `operations coordinator`
   - `content moderator`
   - `data entry`
   - `sales development representative`
3. In the **location** box type **Canada** (or **Ontario, Canada**).
4. Open **Filters** (top bar) and set:
   - **Remote** → On-site/Remote/Hybrid: tick **Remote**
   - **Date posted** → **Past 24 hours** (this is the big one — you want fresh
     postings before 500 people apply)
   - **Experience level** → tick **Internship**, **Entry level**, and
     **Associate**
   - **Sort by** → **Most recent** (not "Most relevant")
5. Hit **Show results**.

## 2. Turn each search into an email alert

At the top of the results there's a **"Set alert"** toggle (or a "Create job
alert" button). Turn it **on**. LinkedIn will email you new matches daily so
you don't have to re-run the search. Do this for each of your role keywords —
you can manage them at **linkedin.com/jobs/job-alerts**.

> This is the manual equivalent of the tracker's daily email: one alert per
> keyword, delivered to your inbox.

## 3. Boolean search (find more, filter junk)

The keyword box understands `AND`, `OR`, `NOT`, quotes, and parentheses. Useful
combos:

- `("customer support" OR "customer success") AND remote`
- `("virtual assistant" OR "administrative assistant") NOT senior`
- `coordinator AND remote NOT manager`

Quotes = exact phrase. `NOT senior` / `NOT manager` strips roles above your
level.

## 4. Applying fast (this is where you win)

- **Sort by Most recent + Past 24 hours**, then apply the same day. Early
  applicants get seen; week-old postings often have hundreds of applicants.
- Prefer **"Apply"** (takes you to the company site) over **"Easy Apply"** when
  you can — fewer people finish a company-site application, so your odds per
  application are better. Easy Apply is fine for volume; just don't *only* do it.
- Keep a saved text file with your standard answers (phone, address, "why this
  role", availability "20–25 hrs/week, ongoing") so each application is 3
  minutes, not 30.
- Paste the job into your tracker's CSV with Status = "not applied", then flip
  it to "applied" once you submit — same habit as the automated rows.

## 5. A couple of LinkedIn-specific edges

- **"Be an early applicant"** label = apply now.
- Turn on **#OpenToWork** (recruiters-only visibility so it's not public on your
  profile) and set the job titles + "Remote" preference — recruiters search this.
- Follow companies that hire remote support/ops at scale; their new posts show
  up in your feed.
- For business-student leverage: message a TMU alum at the company *before* or
  right after applying ("2nd-year TRSM student, applied to your support role,
  would value 10 min of advice"). A warm intro beats a cold application.

## Daily routine (~10 min)

1. Open your LinkedIn job alerts email → apply to anything from the **past 24h**.
2. Check the tracker's CSV / email → apply to the new rows.
3. Aim for 10 applications, mark each "applied", done.
